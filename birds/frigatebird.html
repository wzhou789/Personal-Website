Magnificent Frigatebird

Magnificent Frigatebirds (or man o' war) are large black seabirds with forked tails that live over the tropical and subtropical waters of southern United States, Mexico, and the Caribbean. The male frigatebird has a peculiar red pouch on their throat, which they inflate like a balloon to attract mates. The female frigatebird is larger than the males and has a white breast and belly. Frigatebirds mostly feed on fish caught in flight over the ocean's surface, but the frigatebird is also known as the "man o' war bird" due to feeding habits, in which it sometimes harass other birds until they regurgitate their food.
