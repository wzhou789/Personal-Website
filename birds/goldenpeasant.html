Golden Pheasant

Golden pheasants (also known as the Chinese pheasant) is a gamebird native to western China and later established in many parts of the Americas, Australia, and Europe. The adult male has a long tail two-thirds of its total length, a golden crest and rump, and a bright red body. The female hen is darker and more slender and a tail half its total length. They mainly feed on grains, leaves, and invertebrates on the ground, and roost in trees at night. They are clumsy fliers with short flights and prefer to run.
